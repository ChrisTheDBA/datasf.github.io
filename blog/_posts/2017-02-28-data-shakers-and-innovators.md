On February 16th nearly 100 city analysts packed a room in City Hall for Mayor Edwin Lee’s inaugural Data and Innovation Awards. &nbsp;In November, Mayor Lee sent out a call for the most innovative and data-driven projects City and County employees had completed in the last year. &nbsp;More than 60 submissions were received for the six award categories.
{: .present-before-paste}

We are proud to announce the winners and show off their presentations in full. &nbsp;The presentation skills on display are something else.
{: .present-before-paste}

### If you love your data set it free!

*Made innovative use of open data* ![](/uploads/versions/Election---x----1280-960x---.jpg)
{: .present-before-paste}

For the category 'If you love your data set it free!' the award went to [Ethics Commission](https://sfethics.org/) for their work on the [Campaign Finance Dashboard](https://sfethics.org/ethics/2016/06/campaign-finance-dashboards-june-7-2016-and-november-8-2016-elections.html).&nbsp; The Ethics Commission took traditionally difficult to access campaign finance data and made it accessible via a stylish, creative, and engaging dashboard.

### A Picture is Worth a Thousand Words!

*Produced a beautiful and effective data design or visualization* ![](/uploads/versions/EveryTree---x----1280-960x---.jpg)
{: .present-before-paste}

For the category 'A Picture is Worth a Thousand Words!' the award went to the [Planning Department](http://sf-planning.org/) and [Department of Public Works](http://sfpublicworks.org/) for their work around [EveryTreeSF](http://sf-planning.org/everytreesf-%E2%80%93-street-tree-census). These two departments collaborated to create EveryTreeSF, a [census of street trees](https://www.opentreemap.org/urbanforestmap/map/) in San Francisco. This census will provide the building blocks for developing a long-term plan for maintaining San Francisco’s trees.

### Data Cruncher

*Involved data analysis and number crunching* ![](/uploads/versions/PitStop---x----1280-960x---.jpg)
{: .present-before-paste}

For the category 'Data Cruncher' the award went to the [Controller’s Office](http://sfcontroller.org/) for their work around the Pit Stop Program.&nbsp; They created a rigorous and replicable analytic process for identifying locations that are most in need of a Pit Stop station, ensuring that City resources are used efficiently and effectively.

### User’s First

*This project made use of Human Centered Design* ![](/uploads/versions/ENV---x----1280-960x---.jpg)

### Bridge Builder

*Involved successful collaboration with different departments or external groups* ![](/uploads/versions/DataPortal---x----1280-960x---.jpg)
{: .present-before-paste}

For the category 'Bridge Builder' the award went to the [Planning Department](http://sf-planning.org/), the [Office of Economic and Workforce Development](http://oewd.org/), and the [Public Health](https://www.sfdph.org/dph/default.asp) for their work on the Central Market/Tenderloin Data Portal.&nbsp; By creating the Central Market/Tenderloin Data Portal, these departments brought about collective impact and better collaboration and coordination in the Central Market/Tenderloin neighborhood.
